Our Story

Like most great love stories, ours begins with a theme party and online dating. Cassady’s friend Andrea (aka the Maid of Honor) was tired of hearing horror stories about guys from bars, so she set up an online profile for Cassady without her knowledge. Upon finding out, Cassady cursed Andrea’s first born and drove straight over to punch her in the teeth, only to spend the next 3 hours ogling shirtless men in fedoras. And then, Cassady received this message:

It's funny you put marriage therapy and working towards world peace in the same sentence. I think that if everyone were in a healthy relationship there would be a lot less people out there trying to start wars.

Anyways, you said you like theme parties, and my friend is throwing a great one this Saturday for her 30th birthday. It's a 50 Shades of Grey theme. If that's not too intimidating I'd be happy to invite you.

He didn’t have to wait long for a response:

Hi Austin, I'm Cassady. I don't do a whole lot of marriage counseling, but I have a feeling if Fidel Castro had had a healthy relationship with a good woman, we'd all still be smoking Cubans right now. Maybe that whole missile crisis thing could have been avoided all together.

As for this party you speak of--if by your "friend" you mean you and by "throwing a great party for her 30th birthday" you mean lurking around someone else's house clutching a blindfold and a role of duct tape, well, at least it would make for an interesting first date story. That being said, I'm not going to pretend that I'm not intrigued by your offer…

Cassady received Austin’s reply the next day:

I can see the headlines now:

In a surprising move, on the first day of the 2013 World Peace Summit world leaders were instructed to pair off with their significant others and a licensed therapist so they could engage in behavioral couples therapy and situational role play exercises. The day ended abruptly following these sessions when all of the couples retired to their rooms and were not seen until morning. While interactions between leaders at the start of the summit were tense and argumentative, by the second day the atmosphere was relaxed and friendly and there were a surprising number of winks and high-fives observed. When asked to comment President Obama was quoted as saying "Now that Michelle and I have learned to be happy together I don't feel like I need to prove myself to her anymore, so I'm pulling the troops out of Afghanistan and bringing them home." In a speech following the summit Iranian President Hassan Rouhani said "After learning that my wife loves me for who I am, I came to realize that my attempts to develop nuclear weapons were simply me overcompensating for my own perceived inadequacy. Our nuclear weapons program will be terminated immediately." For his role in masterminding the summit, Dr. Phil has been nominated for the 2013 Nobel Peace Prize.

Unfortunately my friend really does exist, so we'll have to find another way to make for an interesting first date story. She's renting the Red Velvet Room at Rendezvous, so we'll have our own private area but we can also go down to the main area if we want. And don't worry it's all normal people, I don't want you thinking that you're showing up to some kind of swingers party.

Let me know what you think, I promise you'll at least get an interesting first date story out of it.

Cassady was quick to answer:

So I cut and pasted your headline paragraph above onto my cohort's fb page and you were very well received. In fact, two very attractive (albeit taken--sorry!) ladies have "liked" your "article" lol.

I do have to warn you I might not be able to make it because I haven't been feeling well and I have a doctor's appointment tomorrow. I don't want to smoosh your chances to seduce another young lady so if there's someone else you'd like to invite, that's totally cool. I'd hate for you to be forced to waste such a golden opportunity!

Austin’s response was perfect:

Well, I guess it's a good thing that a third attractive and available lady also liked the article ;). I think I'm willing to take my chances and hope that you're feeling better by Saturday. I'm sure I'll have fun there even if you can't make it, but it'll be a lot more fun if you can.

A few Sounders’ games, Jung’s theory on synchronicity, a considerable number of hours binge watching “Iron Chef” on Netflix and a cat stroller later, we arrived in Hawaii where Austin proposed. Cassady immediately said yes!

Wedding FAQ

We’re so glad you asked.

Where do babies come from?

We recommend a great picture book by Robie H. Harris and Michael Emberly called “It’s Not The Stork”. The open, colorful, fun illustrations and authoritative yet friendly tone provide accessibility without over-simplifying the… oh, you meant about the wedding. Okay…

How do I get to the wedding?

Both the ceremony and the reception will take place at Chateau DeLille Cellars, which is right on the boarder of Redmond and Woodinville, Washington, so get excited. The address is 14208 Woodinville-Redmond Road, Northeast, Redmond, WA 98052.

Please note: DO NOT just use google maps or Siri to find “DeLille Cellars” because the interwebs will take you to the wrong place. DeLille has both a tasting room (where there will be no wedding) and a chateau (where there will be an AWESOME wedding). Although these two locations are not far from each other, a mile may as well be 20 if you’re wearing high heels (or carrying a full-grown woman on your back) so please make sure you enter the exact address from above into your GPS or follow these directions to ensure that you don’t miss happy hour!

How far is the venue from the airport? From Seattle?

With normal human driving hovering slightly above the posted speed limit, the venue is about 45 minutes to an hour north of Sea-Tac airport and 30 minutes north of downtown Seattle. In addition to its coffee, rain and militant recycle/compost population, Seattle has no shortage of traffic so please plan accordingly!

I’m a planner. What is your timeline for the big day?

530pm: Happy hour with drinks and apps, so go ahead and leave early! Your boss won’t miss you, unless you are the boss in which case who cares when you leave?
600: Ceremony begins.
630: First kiss.
631: Self-guided tour of adjacent alpaca farm for children (they’ll be fine) and childlike adults (no judgment). The rest of us will be snackin' and sippin’.
715: Dinner.
800: Toasts.
830: Cake, but mostly ice ceam. Dancing. Repeat.
1030: Last call/lock it up...
1100: Grand farewell/sparkler time!

Open bar?

Fancy beer, award-winning DeLille wine (the D2 is particularly phenomenal) and two specialty cocktails of the bride and groom’s choosing will be provided.

Parking details?

Once you turn into the driveway and go up the small hill to the chateau, you’ll make a right and park in the grassy lot. You may leave your car overnight at your own risk, but you must retrieve it by 11am the following day (Saturday) unless you park it with a valet, in which case it won’t matter because we are not providing valet service and you will have just been robbed.

What should I wear?

Dress is semiformal—and not Seattle semiformal, so guys, you will have to suck it up and put on actual pants. Ladies, the open bar dictates cocktail dresses but we do suggest some kind of stretchy bicycle shorts underneath for breakdance fighting. Above all else, remember, photos taken at this event will be utilized aggressively by Janet (the fierce MOB) in family newsletters, emails and photo mugs, so make it look good.

Please note: There are gravel paths leading to the ceremony, which will take place on grass, so flats or wedge shoes are most practical. If you wear spiked heels, we do reserve the right to laugh and point if the terrain takes you down [enter yet another use for full- coverage spandex].

I’m vegetarian. Will there be anything I can eat?

There are quite a few of you out there whom we love that do not partake in meat-eating so rest assured, there will be an entrée option for you. However, if you have made the life choice to forgo animal productions all together, we will warn you butter and bacon will be heavily featured throughout the wedding feast. Therefore it is likely that the only thing you’ll be able to eat is the seasonal vegan flavor from Molly Moon’s Ice Cream (which will be served in place of cake). Well, that and booze.

Will your cats be there?

Umm duh. Sir Snuggles of Fluffington and Lady Gigi, both Siberian pure-breeds, are scheduled to appear as the Best Boy and the Feline of Honor, respectively. The only occurrence barring them from attending the ceremony is a nation-wide shortage of feline anti-anxiety medication and/or Thundershirt™ pet products, and even then, Canada is really, really close. Also, if they just don’t want to. Fingers crossed, the mood is right!

Should I get you presents?

Yes please! We mean, if you’d like to. For the traditionalists, we’ve registered for a few goodies at Macy’s. We’re mostly covered as far as pots and pans go, but with your incredible generosity (and a little too much wine before you fill out the amount on our Honeyfund page), maybe we can stay in that little hut on the water we’ve always dreamed of. It’s amazing how expensive a room with only three walls is.

Where are you going on your honeymoon?

St. Lucia. More details to come...

Where can I stay?

What is there to do?

Believe it or not, Woodinville has quite a bit to do (and most of it includes alcohol in some form). Tour several famous Washington wineries, including Chateau St Michelle and Columbia to name a few, most of which are within walking distance to each other (including the Chateau DeLille). Stop by Redhook Brewery for the ropes course and some microbrews, or get a massage at Willows Lodge. There are also several beautiful trails for those who like to hike, bike and explore nature! Kayaking, rafting and hot air ballooning are other great options.

But why create an exhaustive list here when the nearby Willows Lodge already has one ready to go?
