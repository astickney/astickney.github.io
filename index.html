Our Story

Like most great love stories, ours begins with a theme party and online dating. Cassady’s friend Andrea (aka the Maid of Honor) was tired of hearing horror stories about guys from bars, so she set up an online profile for Cassady without her knowledge. Upon finding out, Cassady cursed Andrea’s first born and drove straight over to punch her in the teeth, only to spend the next 3 hours ogling shirtless men in fedoras. And then, Cassady received this message:

It's funny you put marriage therapy and working towards world peace in the same sentence. I think that if everyone were in a healthy relationship there would be a lot less people out there trying to start wars.

Anyways, you said you like theme parties, and my friend is throwing a great one this Saturday for her 30th birthday. It's a 50 Shades of Grey theme. If that's not too intimidating I'd be happy to invite you.

He didn’t have to wait long for a response:

Hi Austin, I'm Cassady. I don't do a whole lot of marriage counseling, but I have a feeling if Fidel Castro had had a healthy relationship with a good woman, we'd all still be smoking Cubans right now. Maybe that whole missile crisis thing could have been avoided all together.

As for this party you speak of--if by your "friend" you mean you and by "throwing a great party for her 30th birthday" you mean lurking around someone else's house clutching a blindfold and a role of duct tape, well, at least it would make for an interesting first date story. That being said, I'm not going to pretend that I'm not intrigued by your offer…

Cassady received Austin’s reply the next day:

I can see the headlines now:

In a surprising move, on the first day of the 2013 World Peace Summit world leaders were instructed to pair off with their significant others and a licensed therapist so they could engage in behavioral couples therapy and situational role play exercises. The day ended abruptly following these sessions when all of the couples retired to their rooms and were not seen until morning. While interactions between leaders at the start of the summit were tense and argumentative, by the second day the atmosphere was relaxed and friendly and there were a surprising number of winks and high-fives observed. When asked to comment President Obama was quoted as saying "Now that Michelle and I have learned to be happy together I don't feel like I need to prove myself to her anymore, so I'm pulling the troops out of Afghanistan and bringing them home." In a speech following the summit Iranian President Hassan Rouhani said "After learning that my wife loves me for who I am, I came to realize that my attempts to develop nuclear weapons were simply me overcompensating for my own perceived inadequacy. Our nuclear weapons program will be terminated immediately." For his role in masterminding the summit, Dr. Phil has been nominated for the 2013 Nobel Peace Prize.

Unfortunately my friend really does exist, so we'll have to find another way to make for an interesting first date story. She's renting the Red Velvet Room at Rendezvous, so we'll have our own private area but we can also go down to the main area if we want. And don't worry it's all normal people, I don't want you thinking that you're showing up to some kind of swingers party.

Let me know what you think, I promise you'll at least get an interesting first date story out of it.

Cassady was quick to answer:

So I cut and pasted your headline paragraph above onto my cohort's fb page and you were very well received. In fact, two very attractive (albeit taken--sorry!) ladies have "liked" your "article" lol.

I do have to warn you I might not be able to make it because I haven't been feeling well and I have a doctor's appointment tomorrow. I don't want to smoosh your chances to seduce another young lady so if there's someone else you'd like to invite, that's totally cool. I'd hate for you to be forced to waste such a golden opportunity!

Austin’s response was perfect:

Well, I guess it's a good thing that a third attractive and available lady also liked the article ;). I think I'm willing to take my chances and hope that you're feeling better by Saturday. I'm sure I'll have fun there even if you can't make it, but it'll be a lot more fun if you can.

A few Sounders’ games, Jung’s theory on synchronicity, a considerable number of hours binge watching “Iron Chef” on Netflix and a cat stroller later, we arrived in Hawaii where Austin proposed. Cassady immediately said yes!
